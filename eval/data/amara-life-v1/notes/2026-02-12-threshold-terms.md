Had a productive but slightly tense call with [Priya](people/priya-patel) today about the threshold terms we're seeing in later-stage climate deals. She's pushing back on our standard 2x participating preferred, arguing it creates misaligned incentives when founders are already capital-constrained.

She's not wrong. I've been wrestling with this internally. The participating preferred structure made sense when we were protecting against downside in uncertain markets, but we're now in a position where the best founders have options. If we're too aggressive on liquidation preferences, we're selecting for desperate teams rather than exceptional ones.

Priya suggested a threshold conversion model—participating up to 3x, then converting to common. It's elegant. Captures our downside protection while giving founders meaningful upside alignment beyond a certain exit value.

I need to run scenarios on this against our last three portfolio companies. My intuition says the math works, but I want to see it before I bring this to the Monday partners meeting.
