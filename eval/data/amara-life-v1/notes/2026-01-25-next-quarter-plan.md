Q1 planning always feels like trying to predict weather patterns six months out—which, given our climate focus, is a fitting metaphor.

Met with [Elena Rossi](people/elena-rossi) yesterday to align on thesis refinement. We're seeing a clear convergence: the most interesting deals aren't pure climate or pure AI anymore. They're the unsexy infrastructure layers that make both work—grid orchestration software, sensor networks for methane monitoring, compute scheduling for intermittent renewable power. The founders building in these spaces tend to be systems thinkers rather than domain evangelists, which I find refreshing.

For Q2, I want us to go deeper on energy-aware data centers. The hyperscalers are scrambling, and there's an opening for startups that can help them hit sustainability commitments without sacrificing performance. Elena flagged three early-stage teams worth tracking.

Also need to carve out time for LP updates—our climate narrative resonated last year, but I want to sharpen the AI infrastructure angle before the annual meeting. Storytelling matters as much as returns at this stage.
