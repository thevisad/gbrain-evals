Spent the morning reviewing [NovaMind](companies/novamind)'s latest technical documentation after our second meeting last week. I'm increasingly convinced their approach to neuromorphic inference chips could be transformative for edge AI in climate monitoring applications.

[Chen Wei](people/chen-wei) walked me through their power consumption benchmarks—if accurate, they're achieving 3x efficiency gains over current solutions. That matters enormously when you're talking about deploying thousands of sensors across remote agricultural regions or ocean buoy networks.

My hesitation remains around their go-to-market timeline. They're projecting commercial availability by Q4 2026, but the semiconductor supply chain complexities seem underestimated. I've seen this pattern before with hardware startups—brilliant technology, optimistic timelines.

Called two references today. Both praised the founding team's technical depth but flagged concerns about their manufacturing partnerships. Need to dig deeper there.

Planning to bring this to the full partnership meeting next Thursday. The climate infrastructure angle is compelling enough that I want collective input before we move to term sheet discussions.
