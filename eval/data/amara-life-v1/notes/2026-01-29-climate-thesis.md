Spent the morning revisiting our climate thesis with [Derek](people/derek-chen) and I'm increasingly convinced we need to sharpen our focus. The generalist climate funds are getting crushed right now—too much capital chasing the same solar and battery deals with compressed returns.

Where I see real alpha: the intersection of climate infrastructure and AI workloads. Data centers are projected to consume 8% of US electricity by 2028. That's not a trend, it's a structural shift. The companies solving thermal management, grid flexibility, and carbon-aware compute scheduling are going to be essential, not nice-to-have.

[Maria](people/maria-santos) flagged a startup last week doing dynamic load balancing for hyperscalers—routing compute to wherever renewable generation is peaking. Elegant approach. Not sure about the team yet, but the thesis is sound.

My worry is timing. We're early enough that enterprise buyers are still treating this as sustainability theater rather than operational necessity. Need to find founders who can survive the education cycle.
