Spent the morning revisiting our climate thesis with [David Chen](people/david-chen) and I keep coming back to the same tension: the most impactful interventions in carbon removal and grid optimization are increasingly dependent on AI infrastructure that barely exists at scale.

We're not just investing in climate anymore—we're betting on the convergence. The founders who understand both the thermodynamics *and* the transformer architectures are vanishingly rare, but they're the ones building decade-defining companies.

[Maria Santos](people/maria-santos) pushed back on this framing last week, arguing we're overcomplicating the thesis. Maybe. But every deal I've seen in the last quarter that genuinely excited me sat at this intersection: adaptive load balancing, satellite-driven MRV, process optimization for green hydrogen.

I need to write this up more formally for the LP update. The "climate + AI infra" positioning felt contrarian eighteen months ago. Now it feels obvious, which probably means we're entering a crowded phase. Time to sharpen our edge.
