Had a long call with [Elena Rossi](people/elena-rossi) this morning about what she's calling "orange-mode" operations at GridSync. Basically a middle state between normal ops and full emergency protocols—when the grid is stressed but not yet failing. She's convinced this is where the real value creation happens for AI-managed systems.

I keep turning this over. Most founders pitch me on either the steady-state efficiency gains or the crisis-response heroics. Elena's focused on the liminal space. The hours before a blackout that never happens because the system anticipated and adapted.

It's harder to quantify, which makes it harder to sell to LPs. But I think she's onto something fundamental about how resilient infrastructure actually works. Prevention doesn't make headlines, but it's where the compounding value lives.

Need to dig into their telemetry data from the February cold snap. If the numbers support the thesis, this could reshape how we evaluate the whole portfolio's climate resilience stack.
