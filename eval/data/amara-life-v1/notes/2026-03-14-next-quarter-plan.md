Q2 is shaping up to be our most active quarter yet, and I want to make sure we're being intentional about where we spend our time.

After reviewing our pipeline with [Sarah](people/sarah-chen) yesterday, we agreed on three focus areas: (1) grid-edge intelligence—there's a wave of startups applying foundation models to demand response and we're underweight here; (2) industrial decarbonization, specifically cement and steel where the policy tailwinds are finally materializing; (3) climate data infrastructure, which keeps coming up in every diligence process we run.

I'm also pushing us to be more disciplined about saying no earlier. We've been taking too many second meetings on deals that don't fit our thesis. Sarah suggested we implement a 48-hour flag system—if neither of us can articulate a clear reason for conviction within two days of an intro call, we pass.

The market is getting frothy again in climate-adjacent AI. We need to stay sharp on what's genuinely defensible versus what's riding the hype cycle. More thoughts after the LP meeting next week.
