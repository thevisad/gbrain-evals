Spent most of today working through what [Tomoko](people/tomoko-sato) has been calling "orange mode" — that liminal phase where a portfolio company isn't quite in crisis, but the early warning signals are blinking. Not red yet. Just orange.

She walked me through her framework over coffee: cash runway still above six months, but burn rate creeping up; customer churn not alarming in isolation, but the trend line slopes wrong; founder still responsive, but the weekly updates are getting shorter.

The instinct is to wait for clearer data. But by the time you have certainty, your options have narrowed considerably. Tomoko's point is that orange mode is precisely when board members should be leaning in — asking harder questions, stress-testing assumptions, maybe even suggesting a preemptive bridge before the founders are desperate.

I'm applying this lens to two companies in our climate portfolio right now. Neither feels urgent. That might be exactly the problem.
