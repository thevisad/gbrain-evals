Had a long call with [Kofi](people/kofi-mensah) this morning about structuring the threshold terms for the Heliogen follow-on. He's pushing for a lower revenue milestone before the second tranche unlocks—$4M ARR instead of our standard $6M for climate infrastructure deals at this stage.

His argument isn't unreasonable. The regulatory tailwinds from the IRA extension mean these companies are hitting inflection points faster than our 2023-era models predicted. If we hold to $6M, we might be leaving relationship capital on the table when founders feel we're being unnecessarily conservative.

But I keep coming back to the same concern: threshold terms exist to protect deployment discipline, not just our downside. When we lower bars, we're also lowering the operational pressure that forces founding teams to prove unit economics before scaling.

Going to suggest a compromise—$5M ARR but with a 90-day trailing requirement instead of point-in-time measurement. Forces consistency without punishing reasonable seasonality. Will draft the term sheet language tonight.
