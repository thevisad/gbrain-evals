Had a lengthy back-and-forth with [Diego Alvarez](people/diego-alvarez) today about threshold-based milestone terms for their Series A. He's pushing for revenue triggers that unlock additional tranches, which I actually appreciate—shows confidence in their trajectory.

The structure we're converging on: initial $8M at close, with two $3M tranches tied to ARR thresholds ($2M and $5M respectively). Diego wanted time-based caps too, arguing that macro conditions outside their control shouldn't penalize them indefinitely. Fair point.

What I keep coming back to is whether these terms create the right incentives. Sometimes founders optimize for hitting arbitrary thresholds rather than building sustainable growth. But in Diego's case, his climate hardware background means he thinks in long cycles anyway. He's not going to chase vanity metrics.

Still need to pressure-test the anti-dilution provisions if they raise a bridge before hitting the second milestone. That's where these structures get messy. Will circle back with legal Monday.
