Had the second call with [NovaM​ind](companies/novamind) today. [Derek](people/derek-lin) joined to pressure-test their inference optimization claims, and I'm glad he did—he caught a few gaps in their benchmarking methodology that I'd glossed over in my initial excitement.

Their core pitch is still compelling: purpose-built silicon for climate modeling workloads, with 40% better energy efficiency than general-purpose GPU clusters. The team has deep Cerebras and Google TPU lineage, which gives me confidence they understand the hardware-software co-design challenge.

But Derek raised the right question: who's the customer? The climate research labs they're targeting have notoriously thin budgets. The pivot to commercial weather prediction services makes sense on paper, but that's a crowded space with well-funded incumbents.

I asked for introductions to two of their pilot customers. If those conversations validate the efficiency claims in real-world deployments, I want to bring this to Monday's partner meeting. Still feels early, but the thesis alignment is strong.
