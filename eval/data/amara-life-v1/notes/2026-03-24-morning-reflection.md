Woke up before my alarm today—5:47 AM, gray light already pushing through the curtains. Made coffee and sat with the quiet for a while. These mornings are rare now.

I've been thinking about pace. The fund moves fast, the founders move faster, and somewhere in between I'm supposed to find clarity. But clarity doesn't come from speed. It comes from stillness, from the gaps between meetings where I can actually hear my own thinking.

Three years ago I was grinding through diligence decks at 2 AM, convinced that was the job. Now I understand the job is judgment, and judgment requires rest. Requires space. Requires mornings like this one where I don't check Slack until after I've finished my cup.

The climate portfolio is at an inflection point. Several companies approaching Series B decisions simultaneously. I need to show up sharp for them, which means protecting these margins of silence when I can find them.
