Woke up before dawn today—couldn't sleep with the Helios term sheet still rattling around in my head. Made coffee and sat by the window watching the city wake up. There's something clarifying about those quiet hours before Slack notifications start rolling in.

Been thinking about why I got into climate investing in the first place. Seven years ago, I was an infrastructure engineer frustrated that capital wasn't flowing to solutions fast enough. Now I'm on the other side, and some days the pace still feels glacial. But then I remember the portfolio we've built—twelve companies that didn't exist five years ago, now collectively avoiding 2.3 million tons of CO2 annually.

[Yusuf](people/yusuf-mensah) texted me a photo of his daughter's science fair project yesterday. She built a miniature carbon capture device "like Auntie Amara's companies." That's the thread I need to hold onto when board meetings get contentious and due diligence feels endless.

Back to the term sheet.
