# Intro Call — Dev Shankar / Halfway Capital

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) opened the call by giving a brief overview of [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s focus — early-stage infrastructure and developer tooling, typically writing first checks at pre-seed and seed. She noted the fund is currently deploying from Fund III and has been particularly active in the data pipeline and observability space over the last eighteen months.

[Dev Shankar](people/dev-shankar) introduced himself and walked through his background — previously an engineering lead at a mid-stage logistics company, then a two-year stint building internal tooling at a larger cloud provider before deciding to go independent. He's now about four months into building what he described as a "schema-aware data contract layer" that sits between producers and consumers in modern data stacks, targeting data engineering teams at mid-market companies.

Amara asked about the current state of the product. Dev confirmed there's a working prototype, two design partners he's been iterating with on a weekly basis, and early signal that both partners would convert to paying customers within the next quarter. He has not yet incorporated but is planning to do so in the next few weeks ahead of raising a small pre-seed round.

The conversation touched on competitive positioning — Dev named a few established players in the data quality space and articulated why his approach differs at the schema contract layer rather than the observation layer. Amara pushed on go-to-market, specifically whether the initial wedge is a bottoms-up developer motion or a top-down data platform sale. Dev acknowledged this is still an open question he's working through.

Amara expressed genuine interest and flagged that [Mina Kapoor](people/mina-kapoor), who she works closely with on technical diligence, would be a useful person for Dev to speak with given her background in data infrastructure.
