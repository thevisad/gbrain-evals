# Introductory Call — Bill Hart / Halfway Capital

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) opened the call with a brief overview of [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s current focus: early-stage B2B software, with particular interest in infrastructure tooling and fintech infrastructure. She noted the fund is roughly 18 months into its current vehicle and still actively deploying.

[Bill Hart](people/bill-hart) introduced himself as the co-founder and CEO of [Loopline](companies/loopline), a workflow automation platform targeting mid-market operations teams. He gave a high-level product walkthrough — Loopline sits between a company's CRM and data warehouse, surfacing process bottlenecks and recommending automations without requiring engineering involvement. Current ARR is approximately $1.4M, growing around 15% month-over-month over the last quarter. Team is 11 people, mostly engineers, based in Austin.

Amara asked about the competitive landscape relative to tools like Zapier and Make. Bill acknowledged the surface-level similarity but argued Loopline differentiates on depth of analytics and its ability to handle conditional branching logic that no-code tools handle poorly at scale. He mentioned a handful of reference customers, including a logistics firm and a regional insurance broker, both of which have expanded significantly since onboarding.

The conversation touched briefly on go-to-market. Bill's current motion is outbound-heavy, with a small inside sales team. He acknowledged that PLG is on the roadmap but not a near-term priority given the sales cycle length in mid-market.

Amara flagged that [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital) would want to see a more detailed breakdown of net revenue retention and churn before moving forward, and suggested a follow-up with her colleague [Marcus Reid](people/marcus-reid), who leads technical diligence on the platform side.
