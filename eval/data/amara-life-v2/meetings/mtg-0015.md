# Intro Call — Sarah Chen / Halfway Capital

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) and [Sarah Chen](people/sarah-chen) connected for a first-time intro call. Sarah is currently a Series A-stage founder exploring early conversations with investors ahead of a formal raise expected in Q1 2026. The meeting was a warm intro through the broader network.

## Overview

Sarah walked through her company, [Loopframe](companies/loopframe), which is building infrastructure tooling for real-time data reconciliation across distributed warehouse environments. She described the core customer pain point as engineering teams spending disproportionate time debugging sync failures between production pipelines and analytics layers. Loopframe's approach automates detection and resolution at the connector level.

Amara asked about current traction — Sarah noted they have six paying design partners, MRR just crossed $28K, and average contract size is trending toward $60K ARR. The team is eight people, four of whom are engineers.

Amara expressed genuine interest in the space and mentioned that [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital) has been actively looking at data infrastructure plays, particularly anything touching reliability and observability. She noted some overlap in thesis with prior portfolio work but said Loopframe felt differentiated on the reconciliation angle specifically.

There was a brief discussion about competitive positioning relative to larger players like Fivetran and Airbyte. Sarah's view is that those tools optimize for ingestion volume, not correctness guarantees — she sees Loopframe sitting in a distinct layer.
