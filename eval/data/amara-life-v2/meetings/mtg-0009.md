# Intro Call — Dev Shankar / Halfway Capital

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) opened the call by giving a brief overview of [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s thesis — early-stage B2B software, typically pre-Series A, with a focus on infrastructure and workflow tooling. She noted the fund is currently deploying from its second vehicle.

[Dev Shankar](people/dev-shankar) introduced himself and his company, an AI-assisted compliance monitoring platform targeting mid-market financial services firms. He described the core product as a real-time surveillance layer that ingests communications data and flags potential regulatory violations before they escalate. The company is roughly 18 months old, has four full-time employees, and recently closed a $1.2M pre-seed round from angels in the fintech space.

Amara asked about current revenue. Dev confirmed two paying pilot customers — both regional broker-dealers — generating approximately $80K ARR, with a third customer expected to sign within the next six weeks. She probed on the sales cycle length, and Dev acknowledged it runs 90–120 days given procurement and legal review requirements at financial institutions.

The conversation shifted to competitive positioning. Amara raised names like [Behavox](companies/behavox) and [NICE Actimize](companies/nice-actimize) as incumbents in the surveillance space. Dev argued his differentiation is deployment speed and a lighter integration footprint, specifically targeting firms that can't afford or operationally support enterprise-grade solutions.

Amara expressed genuine interest but flagged that the sales cycle dynamic and the narrow initial ICP would be key diligence areas. She mentioned she'd want to loop in [Priya Patel](people/priya-patel), who has deep fintech regulatory experience, before moving to a partner meeting.
