# Intro Call — Marcus Reid / Halfway Capital

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) opened the call by giving a brief overview of [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s current focus — early-stage B2B software, typically pre-Series A, with a particular interest in infrastructure and workflow tooling. She noted the fund is actively deploying from its second vehicle and has capacity for three or four more investments this year.

[Marcus Reid](people/marcus-reid) introduced himself and his company, a developer tooling startup building automated code review and documentation pipelines aimed at mid-market engineering teams. He described the core problem: eng teams at 50–200 person companies lose significant time to onboarding and context-switching, and existing tools either target enterprise (too heavy) or individual developers (too lightweight). The product has been in private beta for about five months with eight paying design partners, ARR currently sitting just under $180K.

Amara asked about retention and expansion dynamics. Marcus said net dollar retention across design partners is tracking around 118%, which she flagged as a strong early signal. She also probed on the competitive angle — specifically how the product differentiates from GitHub Copilot's expanding feature surface. Marcus acknowledged the overlap risk but argued their wedge is team-level workflows and institutional knowledge capture, not individual autocomplete.

The conversation turned to fundraise timing. Marcus indicated he is targeting a $4–5M seed round, hoping to close before end of Q4. He has two other conversations in progress but nothing at term sheet stage yet.

Amara said she wants to bring this to [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s weekly pipeline review and will loop in [Priya Patel](people/priya-patel), who covers developer tooling for the partnership.
