# Intro Call — Dev Shankar / Halfway Capital

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) and [Dev Shankar](people/dev-shankar) connected for a first meeting after an intro through [Marcus Reid](people/marcus-reid). Dev is the co-founder and CEO of [Lumen Trace](companies/lumen-trace), an infrastructure analytics startup working on real-time observability tooling for distributed systems.

Dev gave a quick overview of where the company stands: they closed their seed round in **January 2025 at $4.2M**, led by [Arklight Ventures](companies/arklight-ventures), with a handful of angels. They're now starting to think about the Series A conversation, probably Q4 this year or early 2026 depending on ARR trajectory.

Amara asked about current ARR and growth — Dev said they're at roughly $680K ARR, growing about 15% month-over-month for the last three months. Three enterprise pilots converting to paid in June. Team is 11 people, mostly eng.

Amara flagged that [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital) doesn't typically lead seeds but does participate in Series A rounds in the $8–15M range, and that this looked like a plausible fit timing-wise. She mentioned she'd want to loop in [Priya Patel](people/priya-patel) from the platform team if things progress, given Priya's background in dev-tools go-to-market.

Dev asked whether Halfway has existing exposure to the observability space — Amara noted they have one portfolio company adjacent ([Stackform](companies/stackform)) but it's more on the security side, so no direct conflict.
