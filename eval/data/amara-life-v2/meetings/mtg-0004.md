# Introductory LP Conversation — Bill Hart

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) opened the call by giving [Bill Hart](people/bill-hart) a brief overview of [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s thesis: early-stage B2B software, typically pre-Series A, with a focus on operationally complex industries like logistics, healthcare infrastructure, and manufacturing. Fund III is currently in market targeting $180M.

Bill shared that he manages a family office with meaningful exposure to venture already — primarily through two large multi-strategy funds — and is looking to add a more focused, hands-on manager relationship. He mentioned he's done direct co-investments in the past but found deal flow inconsistent without a strong GP anchor.

Amara walked through portfolio construction: roughly 25–28 companies per fund, initial checks of $3–6M, with reserves held back for follow-on into the top third of the portfolio. She emphasized the team's operational backgrounds as a differentiator — several partners came from scaling companies rather than pure finance.

Bill asked specifically about fee structure and whether there was flexibility for larger LP commitments. Amara indicated she could share the standard LPA but noted that meaningful anchor commitments had received modified terms in prior funds. She was careful not to make any specific commitments on the call.

Toward the end, Bill raised interest in co-investment rights as a priority. Amara confirmed that Halfway has historically offered co-invest to LPs on a pro-rata basis for deals above a certain threshold, subject to capacity.
