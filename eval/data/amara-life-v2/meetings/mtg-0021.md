# Portfolio Check-In: Series B Pipeline Review

[Amara Okafor](people/amara-okafor) and [Diego Alvarez](people/diego-alvarez) connected for a scheduled check-in to review [Halfway Capital](companies/halfway-capital)'s current Series B deal pipeline and discuss prioritization heading into Q1 2026.

## Key Discussion Points

**Pipeline Overview**
Diego walked through the current funnel — approximately eleven active opportunities at various stages. Amara flagged three as priority prospects, noting that two of them are likely to move toward term sheets before end of year. The third is earlier in diligence and may slip into January.

**Sector Focus**
The conversation touched on whether Halfway Capital should double down on infrastructure software or broaden into climate tech given inbound interest. Amara expressed measured skepticism about spreading focus too thin, particularly given current fund deployment timelines. Diego agreed but suggested keeping one or two climate-adjacent deals in the pipeline for optionality.

**LP Update Cadence**
Amara noted that the Q4 LP letter is due before the holiday break and asked Diego to pull together updated portfolio metrics — specifically ARR growth and net retention figures — from the existing portfolio companies by December 17th. Diego confirmed he could have those ready.

**Team Capacity**
Brief discussion about bandwidth heading into year-end. Amara mentioned that [Mina Kapoor](people/mina-kapoor) is handling the legal coordination on the two near-term deals and that [Marcus Reid](people/marcus-reid) has been looped in on technical diligence for the infrastructure software prospect.

## Decisions

- Prioritize the two Series B deals likely to close before year-end; defer the third to January review cycle.
- Hold off on formally adding climate tech as a thesis vertical until Q1 strategy offsite.
