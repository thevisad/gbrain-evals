Woke up earlier than intended and ended up sitting with coffee for a full hour before opening my laptop. Rare luxury lately.

Been thinking about the tension I keep running into between pace and conviction. Climate infrastructure deals move slowly — permitting, interconnection queues, offtake negotiations — but the AI energy demand story is accelerating so fast that by the time diligence closes, the underlying assumptions have shifted. It creates this strange pressure where moving carefully feels like a liability.

I don't think the answer is to move faster, though. I think the answer is to get cleaner on which assumptions actually need to hold for a thesis to work, and which ones are just narrative comfort.

That's the real diligence discipline. Not more data — better questions.

Wrote three of them down this morning before the day started pulling in every direction. Going to try to make that a habit. The quiet hour before the noise might be the most productive time I have.
