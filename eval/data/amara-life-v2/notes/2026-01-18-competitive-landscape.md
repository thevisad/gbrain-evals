Spent the better part of this week mapping the competitive landscape across climate-adjacent AI infrastructure. What's striking is how compressed the differentiation window has become. Eighteen months ago, you could point to a handful of genuine moats — proprietary grid datasets, purpose-built inference hardware, long-term utility contracts. Now everyone seems to have a version of that story.

Had a sharp conversation with [Priya Anand](people/priya-anand) about where the real separation happens at the application layer versus the compute layer. Her instinct aligns with mine: the winners will own the workflow, not just the watts.

[Dom Ferrara](people/dom-ferrara) flagged two new entrants out of Europe that I hadn't tracked closely. Worth a deeper look — their demand-forecasting approach is genuinely differentiated.

[Lena Scout](people/lena-scout) pushed back on my framing, arguing incumbents have more runway than we're crediting. She's probably right that I'm discounting switching costs.

My takeaway: we need a sharper conviction framework before Q1 ends. The landscape is moving faster than our diligence cadence.
