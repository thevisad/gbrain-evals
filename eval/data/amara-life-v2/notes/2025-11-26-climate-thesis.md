Spent the morning revisiting our climate thesis ahead of the year-end LP update, and I keep coming back to the same conviction: the energy transition doesn't have a technology problem anymore — it has an infrastructure and coordination problem. The breakthroughs are largely here. What's missing is the capital stack that actually moves fast enough to deploy them at grid scale.

Where I think we've sharpened our view this year is at the intersection with AI infrastructure. Data centers are now a material driver of electricity demand, and that demand is creating both pressure and opportunity — pressure on already strained grids, but also a forcing function for new generation and storage projects that might otherwise have waited another decade for economics to improve.

That's the bet we're making: that the companies building the connective tissue between AI compute demand and clean energy supply will define the next decade of infrastructure. The market hasn't fully priced that convergence yet. We need to move deliberately, but we need to move.
