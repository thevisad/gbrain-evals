Spent most of today buried in the GreenPulse data room. The core thesis still holds — distributed grid sensing married to real-time ML inference is genuinely differentiated — but a few things are nagging at me after the call with [Dev Lindqvist](people/dev-lindqvist) this morning.

Their inference latency numbers look strong in the demo environment, but I'm not convinced the benchmarks translate cleanly to edge hardware in high-humidity conditions. Asked [Priya Anand](people/priya-anand) to pull comparable deployment data from our Volta Grid investment for a rough sanity check.

The revenue model is also still a little soft. They're straddling SaaS and usage-based in a way that could work, but makes forecasting uncomfortable at Series A. Need to understand whether that's intentional flexibility or early-stage indecision before we go further.

Next step is a technical reference call — probably mid-week. If the infrastructure assumptions hold up, I think we have something worth bringing to the full partnership. Cautiously optimistic.
