Spent most of today deep in the GreenPulse data room. Their grid-edge inference architecture is genuinely impressive — running localized demand-forecasting models directly on substation hardware cuts latency and meaningfully reduces the bandwidth burden upstream. That part I believe.

What I'm still turning over is the go-to-market. [Priya Patel](people/priya-patel) flagged something in our debrief that I keep coming back to: the utility sales cycle here could easily stretch 18 months, and GreenPulse's runway math doesn't seem to account for that friction honestly. Their pilot conversion assumptions feel optimistic by maybe a full quarter on average.

I want to bring this back to the partnership as a genuine open question rather than a red flag. The underlying thesis — AI infra purpose-built for energy infrastructure — sits squarely in what we care about. But thesis fit doesn't paper over execution risk. Need to get a reference call scheduled with someone inside PG&E or Eversource who's actually evaluated them. That conversation will tell me more than another hour in the data room.
