Spent most of today deep in the diligence materials for [Jordan Walsh](people/jordan-walsh)'s round. The technical architecture is genuinely impressive — the way they've structured the inference pipeline to minimize idle compute is exactly the kind of efficiency-first thinking I want to see in a climate-adjacent AI infra play. Power consumption projections look credible, which is rare; most founders at this stage are still hand-waving on that number.

My lingering question is around the go-to-market sequencing. Jordan is clearly a builder first, and the enterprise sales motion feels underdeveloped relative to where they'll need to be in twelve months. That's fixable, but I want to pressure-test whether they've internalized *why* it matters, not just that we've flagged it.

Planning a follow-up call Thursday to walk through the customer pipeline together. If the early design-partner conversations are as warm as the deck suggests, I think we move to partner meeting quickly.
