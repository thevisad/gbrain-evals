Early morning, coffee still hot, and I keep coming back to yesterday's conversation with [Dev Shankar](people/dev-shankar). He's still CEO at Meridian Compute — which honestly surprises me given the board pressure I've been hearing whispers about — and he seems genuinely committed to steering the company through this next infrastructure buildout cycle rather than taking an exit. That steadiness matters when we're evaluating whether to lead the Series C.

What struck me most was his framing of the energy cost problem. Not as a constraint to engineer around, but as the actual product thesis. That's rare. Most founders in AI infra treat power as a line item. Dev treats it as leverage.

I need to think carefully about whether Halfway is the right lead here or whether we bring in a co-investor from the utility-adjacent side. Either way, I'm more optimistic this morning than I was walking into that meeting.
