Been spending the last few weeks mapping the competitive landscape more carefully — specifically the overlap between climate infra and AI compute bets. The density of new entrants is real, but I'm not sure the differentiation is. Most funds I'm tracking are chasing the same handful of deals: grid-edge software, liquid cooling, long-duration storage. The thematic framing is almost interchangeable at this point.

Had a useful calibration conversation with [Priya Anand](people/priya-anand) about where genuine white space still exists. Her read on demand-side flexibility platforms aligned with mine — underfunded relative to the actual grid need.

[Tobias Renner](people/tobias-renner) pushed back, arguing the crowding is overstated and that execution variance between teams matters more than positioning. He's probably right that we fixate too much on who else is in a market. Still, I think [Halfway Capital](people/halfway-capital) earns its returns by being early and specific, not by being the best player in a crowded room. Staying disciplined on that matters more now than it did twelve months ago.
