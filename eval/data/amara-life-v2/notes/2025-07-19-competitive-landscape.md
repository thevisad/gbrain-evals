Spent the afternoon mapping where the real white space is in climate AI infra. Honestly, the landscape feels more crowded than it did eighteen months ago, but I'm not sure the crowding is meaningful — a lot of capital is chasing the same three or four data center cooling narratives without asking harder questions about grid interconnection timelines.

Had a useful call with [Priya Mehta](people/priya-mehta) about how she's seeing differentiation play out on the hardware side. Her read aligns with mine: the durable moats are going to be in software that can adapt dispatch logic to increasingly volatile spot pricing, not in the physical layer itself.

[Tom Wentzel](people/tom-wentzel) pushed back on that framing last week, arguing that vertical integration still wins. I respect the thesis but I think he's underweighting regulatory fragmentation across WECC and PJM.

Net takeaway: we should tighten our sourcing criteria around grid-aware inference optimization. That's where I want to spend the next quarter.
