Been thinking a lot lately about what I'm calling "orange mode" — that particular mental state where urgency and clarity converge without tipping into panic. It's rare, and I've been trying to cultivate it more deliberately in how I approach deals.

The climate infrastructure space right now demands it. We're in a window — probably 18 to 36 months — where the AI power demand story and the clean energy buildout story are genuinely overlapping in ways that create real investment leverage. If you're moving too slow, you miss the structure. If you're moving in full red-alert mode, you make sloppy commitments.

Orange mode is the middle register. Focused, a little accelerated, but still reasoning carefully.

I've started blocking Tuesday mornings specifically for orange-mode thinking — no calls, no Slack, just reading and writing. It's already changing how I frame thesis memos. Small habit, but I think it compounds. Wanted to document it here before I convince myself it's obvious in hindsight.
