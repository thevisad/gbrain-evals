Been spending the first weeks of January revisiting our climate thesis ahead of the LP update. A few things feel more durable than ever: the convergence of AI infrastructure demand with grid constraints is not a temporary dislocation — it's structural. Every hyperscaler buildout we're seeing accelerates the case for flexible load, long-duration storage, and behind-the-meter generation.

What's shifting for me is the geographic lens. I used to anchor heavily on the US market, but the permitting reform tailwinds in parts of Europe and the raw renewable resource potential across West Africa are starting to deserve serious attention in our framework.

The harder question I'm sitting with: how do we avoid backing climate solutions that only pencil out if carbon prices stay elevated? Our best investments have to work on economics alone, with climate impact as the multiplier — not the crutch.

Going to pressure-test this framing with the team before we finalize the deck. I want our thesis to be honest about where we're still uncertain.
