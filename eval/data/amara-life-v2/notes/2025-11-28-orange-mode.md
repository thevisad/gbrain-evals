Thanksgiving week always puts me in what I've started calling "orange mode" — that particular state where the year's urgency softens just enough to actually think. Fewer pings, fewer term sheets demanding attention by end of day. I used to resist it, feeling like I was falling behind. Now I protect it.

Spent part of this morning reviewing notes from my conversation last week with [Jordan Park](people/jordan-park) about the thermal storage space. There's something there worth sitting with — the gap between where grid operators say they want to be and what procurement timelines actually reflect. Orange mode is exactly when I can hold that tension without rushing toward a conclusion.

The best investments I've been part of originated in this kind of slow-motion reflection, not in frenzied Q4 dealmaking. I want to carry that patience into December rather than letting the calendar anxiety creep back in as soon as the holiday ends. A small discipline, but a meaningful one.
