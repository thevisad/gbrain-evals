Early morning, coffee still too hot to drink. I've been sitting with the question of whether we're moving fast enough — not just as a fund, but as an ecosystem. The climate window keeps narrowing while the AI infrastructure buildout accelerates in parallel, and I keep asking myself whether those two curves are actually converging toward something good or just creating a more efficient version of the same extractive patterns.

What gives me genuine optimism this week: the founders I've been talking to aren't treating climate and compute as separate conversations anymore. That felt almost impossible to broker eighteen months ago. Now it's the starting assumption in almost every pitch that lands on my desk.

Still, I worry we're funding solutions faster than we're funding understanding. Need to sit with that longer. Maybe that's what these Thursday mornings are for — slowing down enough to notice what the spreadsheets can't capture.
