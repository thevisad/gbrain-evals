Spent most of this week buried in GreenPulse's data room. Initial reaction: the core thesis is sound — using ML inference at the grid edge to shave peak demand is exactly the kind of climate-AI convergence we want exposure to. [Dev Vasiliev](people/dev-vasiliev) has built something technically credible here, and the pilot numbers out of the Ontario microgrid are genuinely encouraging.

That said, I have real questions about unit economics at scale. The hardware margin assumptions feel optimistic, and I'm not convinced the inference costs are modeled correctly post-2027 when the competitive landscape for edge chips gets messier. Flagged this for [Priya Anand](people/priya-anand) to stress-test with a few reference customers next week.

Still bullish enough to keep moving. If the customer conversations hold up, I'd want to bring this to the partnership by mid-February. The window here is real — two other funds are clearly circling.
