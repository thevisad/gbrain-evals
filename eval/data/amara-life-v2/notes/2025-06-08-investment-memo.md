Spent most of today drafting the investment memo for the grid-scale battery storage company we've been tracking since Q1. The numbers are genuinely compelling — declining cell costs plus rising grid interconnection demand create a window that feels real, not manufactured. What keeps giving me pause is the permitting timeline risk; we've seen too many hardware plays with solid unit economics get stranded at the interconnection queue stage.

I want to make sure the memo captures that asymmetry honestly rather than burying it in the risk section. Good memos don't just justify the investment — they steel-man the bear case so the partnership can make a clear-eyed call.

Also flagging that the AI inference load growth angle deserves its own section. Data center power demand is now a first-order input to grid investment theses, not a footnote. If we're right about GPU cluster buildout continuing through 2027, the demand side of this equation looks even more durable than the headline model suggests. Planning to circulate a draft to the full team by end of week.
