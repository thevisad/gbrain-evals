Spent most of this morning working through the Q4 climate-tech market reports that landed over the break. The numbers are broadly in line with what we were expecting — cooling in late-stage valuations, but early-stage deal flow held up better than the headlines suggested. What caught my attention was the divergence between pure-play climate hardware and the AI infrastructure plays with a credible decarbonization angle. The latter are still commanding real premiums.

Had a good call with [Omar Sheikh](people/omar-sheikh) this afternoon to compare notes. He's seeing similar signals on the infrastructure side and flagged a few grid-edge compute deals worth watching in Q1. I want to dig deeper into the demand-response angle there — it feels underappreciated.

Overall, my read is that 2026 opens with more discipline than 2025, which is probably healthy. Founders who can speak fluently to both the climate thesis and the operational efficiency story are going to have an easier time. That bar feels right to me.
