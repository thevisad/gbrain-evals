Woke up early again, which has been the pattern lately. There's something clarifying about the hour before the calendar fills up — before the term sheets and the LP calls and the endless Slack threads about whether a given data center can hit its PUE targets.

I've been thinking about why I ended up at the intersection of climate and AI infrastructure specifically. It wasn't accidental, but it also wasn't as deliberate as I sometimes make it sound in pitches. It was more like following the heat — literally and figuratively. The places where compute demand is straining the grid are exactly the places where the old energy assumptions are breaking down fastest.

That tension is where interesting companies get born.

I need to remember to hold onto that framing when I'm deep in diligence and everything feels like spreadsheets. The deals that have mattered most to me weren't just financially sound — they pointed toward something. I want to keep asking whether a given founder is pointing toward something real.

Coffee's gone cold. Back to it.
