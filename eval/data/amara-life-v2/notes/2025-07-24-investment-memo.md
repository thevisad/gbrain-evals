Spent the better part of today stress-testing the unit economics on a compute cooling deal we've been circling for six weeks. The numbers are holding — PUE down to 1.12 on their latest pilot build, which is genuinely impressive for a modular liquid-cooling stack at that scale. What keeps pulling me back is how cleanly it sits at the intersection of our two theses: AI infrastructure demand isn't softening, and the grid pressure from that demand is very real. Operators are going to need this layer whether they like it or not.

Still a few things I want to pressure-test before we go to IC. Their go-to-market leans heavily on hyperscaler relationships that aren't fully contracted yet — that's a risk I'm not ready to hand-wave away. And I want a second conversation with the CTO about their manufacturing partnership; single-source dependencies at this stage make me nervous.

Overall sentiment is cautiously optimistic. If the next diligence call lands the way I'm hoping, I think we move to term sheet by end of August.
