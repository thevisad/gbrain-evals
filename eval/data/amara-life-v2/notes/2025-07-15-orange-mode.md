Had a long call with [Elena Rossi](people/elena-rossi) this afternoon about what she's calling "orange-mode" — her internal shorthand for the operational state where a climate infrastructure company is technically viable but commercially fragile. Revenue exists, unit economics are improving, but one bad quarter or one delayed offtake agreement could reset everything.

It's a useful frame. I've been thinking about how many of our portfolio companies are sitting exactly there right now. The AI-adjacent energy projects especially — demand signals look strong, but the contracting timelines don't match the burn rates.

Elena's instinct is to lean into bridge structures rather than forcing a Series B before the metrics are clean. I'm inclined to agree. Rushing a raise in orange-mode tends to lock in valuation anchors that become painful later.

What I want to map out this week: which of our companies are genuinely approaching green-mode versus which are normalizing orange as a permanent condition. That second category worries me more than any single deal risk.
