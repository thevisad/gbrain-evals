Spent most of today in what I've started calling "orange mode" — that particular shade of high-alert focus where the calendar is full but nothing feels urgent enough to actually deserve it. Three portfolio calls, a term sheet redline, and a long thread about whether grid-scale battery storage still counts as "AI infrastructure" adjacent. (My answer: yes, obviously, if you've seen the load forecasts for new data center buildouts.)

What I keep coming back to is how much of early-stage climate investing is just attention management. The signal-to-noise ratio is brutal. Every week there's a new founder with a genuinely clever idea sitting inside a market that simply isn't ready to absorb it yet.

That gap between technical readiness and market timing is where most of our bets live. It's uncomfortable. It's also, I think, the only honest place to be doing this work right now. Orange mode feels right for the moment.
