Spent most of today pressure-testing the investment memo for the Series B we're tracking in the liquid-cooled data center space. [Priya Subramaniam](people/priya-subramaniam) flagged something I keep returning to: the capex-to-carbon ratio on their current facility design is compelling on paper, but it assumes a grid mix that looks optimistic past 2028. That's a real risk we can't footnote away.

[Cole Whitfield](people/cole-whitfield) pushed back and argued the PPA structure hedges enough of that exposure. He's probably right that it de-risks the near term, but I'm less convinced it changes the fundamental story at scale.

What I keep coming back to is this: the best climate-AI infra bets aren't just about efficiency today — they're about whether the architecture is defensible as grids tighten and regulatory pressure compounds. This one sits right on that line. I want another week with the operational data before we move to IC. Patience here is the discipline.
