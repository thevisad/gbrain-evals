Been refining our climate thesis this week after the [Forthright Energy](people/forthright-energy) diligence wrapped. Still processing it honestly. We led their Series B — closed at $34M in late May — and we took an 18% stake, which felt right given the grid-optimization angle overlaps cleanly with our AI infra conviction. [Priya Venkataraman](people/priya-venkataraman) flagged some demand-side risk I want to revisit before we finalize the memo.

The broader thesis question I keep returning to: are we too early on industrial electrification, or exactly on time? The Forthright deal makes me think the latter. Their model assumes PPA repricing cycles that [Dele Osei](people/dele-osei) thinks are conservative — I'm inclined to agree.

Sitting with the portfolio mix. Climate infra feels less speculative than it did eighteen months ago. That's either vindicating or a sign we should be pushing further out on the risk curve. Probably both.
