The Q3 climate-AI infrastructure report dropped this morning and I've been sitting with it all day. The headline numbers are striking — data center energy demand projections revised upward by nearly 40% from the January forecast — but what's catching my attention is the regional breakdown. The Southeast Asian buildout is accelerating faster than almost anyone modeled.

[Lena Park](people/lena-park) flagged the cooling infrastructure section to me before lunch, and she was right to. The assumptions baked into traditional cooling OpEx feel dangerously stale given where ambient temperature trends are heading. This is exactly the kind of second-order effect that gets missed when energy analysts and climate scientists aren't in the same room.

For our portfolio, this reinforces the thesis on grid-edge compute. The market is starting to price in resilience, not just efficiency. That's a meaningful shift. I want to revisit our exposure to firms that are still optimizing purely for PUE without accounting for climate-adjusted site risk. Worth a partners call this week.
