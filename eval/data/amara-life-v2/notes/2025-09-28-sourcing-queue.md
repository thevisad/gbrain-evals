Spent the better part of this morning clearing out the sourcing queue before end of quarter. A lot of noise this cycle — the usual spray of decks from founders who slapped "AI-native" onto something that's fundamentally just a managed services play.

One exception: [Lena Park](people/lena-park) flagged an interesting pre-seed out of Vancouver doing inference optimization specifically for edge deployments in grid monitoring. The compression angle is genuinely novel, or at least I haven't seen it packaged this way before. Worth a first call.

I'm also noticing a pattern where the strongest climate-infra founders right now are coming out of national lab backgrounds rather than hyperscalers. They think in systems, not products, which tends to matter when your customer is a utility or a grid operator. Keeping that lens sharp as I triage the rest of the queue this week.

Goal is to have a short list for the Monday team meeting. Three names, maybe four.
