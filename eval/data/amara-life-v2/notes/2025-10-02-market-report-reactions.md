Spent most of this morning working through the Q3 climate-tech market report that [Priya Subramaniam](people/priya-subramaniam) flagged last week. The headline numbers are striking — grid-scale storage deployment is up nearly 40% year-over-year, but the financing structures still feel fragile to me. A lot of that growth is riding on tax credit transferability, and I keep wondering how much of it survives a policy wobble in 2026.

On the AI infra side, the data center energy intensity figures confirm what we've been hearing anecdotally. The gap between stated sustainability commitments and actual load growth is widening, not narrowing. [Leon Voss](people/leon-voss) and I were talking through this yesterday — the opportunity is real, but so is the reputational risk for LPs who are watching scope 2 disclosures closely.

My takeaway: the market is moving fast enough that we need sharper conviction on which efficiency plays are durable versus which are just riding the wave. Will bring a tighter framework to next week's IC prep.
