Spent most of today doing a sweep across the portfolio — Q4 numbers are mostly in and the picture is more mixed than I'd like heading into the new year.

[Tariq Hassan](people/tariq-hassan) and the team at GridMind are tracking well; their inference-on-demand contracts for grid balancing are converting faster than we projected in the Series B model. That's the bright spot.

More concerned about [Priya Nambiar](people/priya-nambiar)'s company, Verdant Compute. Cooling costs came in 18% over budget last quarter — the Arizona site is struggling in ways the thermal modeling didn't anticipate. We need a real conversation about capex phasing before they go back to market.

[Lena Schulz](people/lena-schulz) flagged something interesting on the carbon-accounting side: a potential EU regulatory tailwind that could accelerate enterprise adoption. Worth a deeper look in February.

Overall takeaway: the thesis on climate-adjacent AI infrastructure is holding, but execution risk is real and I need to be more hands-on with the mid-stage companies this half.
