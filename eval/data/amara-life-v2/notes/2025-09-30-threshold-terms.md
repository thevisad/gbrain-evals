Been spending the back half of this week revisiting how we structure threshold terms in our climate-AI infra deals. The pattern I keep running into: founders anchoring on revenue multiples that made sense in 2021 but don't hold up against today's capital costs or grid interconnection timelines. We've started insisting on milestone-gated tranches tied to actual infrastructure delivery — not LOIs, not pilot MWs, but energized capacity. It feels stricter on paper, but honestly it protects everyone. A founder who can't hit an interconnection milestone in 24 months probably shouldn't be drawing the next tranche anyway.

The harder conversation is around valuation step-ups at each threshold. I want them meaningful enough to reward execution without creating cliffs that make the cap table unworkable at Series B. Still figuring out the right calibration. Going to bring this to our LP advisory call next week and see if anyone with infrastructure debt experience has a cleaner framework. This feels like one of those structural questions the whole sector needs to solve together rather than each firm reinventing it deal by deal.
