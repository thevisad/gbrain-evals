Had a long walk-and-talk with [Nadia Freeman](people/nadia-freeman) this afternoon, which helped me crystallize something I've been circling for weeks. The climate-AI infrastructure space is starting to feel genuinely crowded in a way it didn't even eighteen months ago. Every major crossover fund seems to have quietly stood up a dedicated vertical, and the deal quality at the early stages is getting picked over fast.

What I keep coming back to is differentiation at the thesis level, not just the portfolio level. Nadia pushed back on my instinct to go narrower — she made a fair point that specialist positioning can cut you out of breakout companies that don't fit a clean category.

I don't fully agree, but I'm holding the tension. Our edge at Halfway has always been the operator network and the willingness to engage on grid interconnection complexity that generalist funds wave away. That's still real. I just need to make sure we're not mistaking domain fluency for actual proprietary access to the best deals.

More to think through here.
