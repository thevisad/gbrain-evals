Woke up early this morning thinking about a conversation I had last week with [Leo Vance](people/leo-vance). He pushed back on something I've been taking for granted — that climate infrastructure and AI infrastructure are naturally converging investment theses. His point was sharper than I initially gave him credit for: the timelines are misaligned. AI buildout is happening in quarters; energy transition happens in decades. That tension is real, and I've been papering over it in my framing.

I still believe the thesis holds, but Leo's challenge forced me to be more precise about *where* the convergence actually lives. It's not the whole stack — it's the middle layer. Power procurement, cooling innovation, grid interconnection. That's where both worlds are genuinely pulling in the same direction right now.

Need to write that up properly before our next LP update. Grateful for skeptics in my orbit.
