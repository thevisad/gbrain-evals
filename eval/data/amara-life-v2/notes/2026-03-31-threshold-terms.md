Had a long call with [Anna Petrov](people/anna-petrov) this afternoon going over threshold terms for the climate infra round. We're at that familiar inflection point where the valuation protection mechanisms start to feel more like landmines than guardrails — full ratchets versus weighted average, the usual tension.

Anna pushed hard on a 2x liquidation preference with participation, which I understand from a downside-protection standpoint, but I think it sends the wrong signal to the team at this stage. They've built something real. Structuring like we don't believe in the upside is corrosive.

Landed on a 1x non-participating preference with a broad-based weighted average anti-dilution. Clean enough that founders don't feel penalized for the next round, protective enough that we're not writing reckless checks.

Still need to align on the pro-rata rights language before the term sheet goes out Thursday. These details compound — worth getting right the first time.
