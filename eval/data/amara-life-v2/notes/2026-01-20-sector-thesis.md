Been sitting with our thesis refresh all week, trying to distill what we actually believe heading into 2026. The through-line keeps coming back to the same thing: climate and AI infrastructure are not two bets — they're one. The compute buildout required for frontier models is an energy buildout by definition. You can't separate them anymore, and I think a lot of generalist funds are still underweighting that coupling.

What excites me most right now is the layer between physical infrastructure and software — thermal management, grid interconnection tooling, demand forecasting at the substation level. Unglamorous, deeply technical, and chronically underfunded.

I want us to be the firm that takes those founders seriously before anyone else does. We've always said patient capital, differentiated access. This is where that actually gets tested.

Writing this partly as a reminder to myself: resist the urge to chase the obvious headline deals. The real edge is in conviction before consensus.
