Been sitting with our climate thesis all morning, trying to articulate why the AI infrastructure angle feels so urgent right now. The short version: compute is the new carbon problem, and it's also the most credible path to solving the old one.

What keeps pulling me back is the compounding dynamic. Every watt we save through smarter grid orchestration or better cooling architecture gets reinvested into the models that find the next efficiency gain. It's not linear. That's what I want Halfway's portfolio to reflect — not clean energy companies on one side and AI infra on the other, but businesses that genuinely sit at the intersection.

The harder question is diligence. How do we actually verify a company's energy claims at the hardware level, not just the marketing deck level? I don't have a clean answer yet. Flagging this for our next IC meeting. Need to build the muscle before deal flow accelerates, which — based on what I'm seeing in my pipeline — it absolutely will.
