Spent the morning in what I've started calling "orange mode" — that restless, half-caffeinated state where everything feels urgent but nothing is quite on fire. Reviewed the latest capacity projections from [Marcus Thill](people/marcus-thill) over at GridNest. He's still CEO there, steering that team through a genuinely tricky interconnection backlog, and I think his steadiness is underrated by the market. The orange-mode energy is actually useful for this kind of read — you catch the anxious subtext in a deck that a calmer morning might gloss over.

What I keep circling back to: the gap between where AI inference load is heading and where firm renewable capacity actually sits in 2026–2027 is not a rounding error. It's a structural problem. Orange mode or not, that's the thesis. We need to get comfortable funding the unglamorous middle layer — storage dispatch, demand flexibility, thermal buffering — before the glamorous stuff makes any sense at all.

Note to self: schedule a proper think with Marcus before end of Q2.
