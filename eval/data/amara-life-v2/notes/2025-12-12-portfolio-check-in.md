Wrapped up my end-of-year portfolio calls today. The one with [Bill Hart](people/bill-hart) stood out — Meridian Grid's thermal storage numbers are genuinely ahead of where I expected them to be at this stage. Their Q4 deployment figures suggest the unit economics are finally tightening in the right direction. I've been watching this space long enough to stay cautious about premature optimism, but something about the operational data feels different this cycle.

On the AI infra side, two of our earlier bets are showing real datacenter energy demand as a tailwind rather than headwind — which is the thesis playing out, though faster than modeled. Need to think carefully about follow-on sizing before the next board cycle in February.

Overall, the portfolio feels more coherent than it did twelve months ago. The overlap between climate infrastructure and compute demand is becoming legible to LPs in a way it simply wasn't before. That clarity matters for the next fund conversation.
