Spent most of the afternoon on LP calls — a quarterly ritual that I find genuinely useful when I go in with the right mindset. [Priya Mehta](people/priya-mehta) from the endowment side pushed hard on our deployment pace, which is fair. We've been deliberate, maybe overly so, but I'd rather defend patience than explain a rushed check into something that doesn't hold up on the climate attribution side.

[Jonah Sterling](people/jonah-sterling) was more philosophical — wanted to understand how we're thinking about the AI infra buildout as both an opportunity and a risk to our climate thesis. Honest question, and one I keep turning over myself. The power demand story cuts both ways.

The through-line I kept coming back to: our LPs signed up for a specific lens, not just returns. Maintaining that trust means being transparent when the market pulls against the thesis, not just when it confirms it. That candor is the relationship.
