Spent the better part of this afternoon on a call with [Dev Anand](people/dev-anand) and [Priya Subramaniam](people/priya-subramaniam) going through CarbonLedger's Q3 numbers. Honestly, more encouraging than I expected. Their edge inference layer is finally pulling accurate emissions attribution at the facility level — something they've been promising since the seed round. Customer retention is holding above 90%, which tells me the product is genuinely sticky now, not just pilot-sticky.

My lingering concern is compute cost. Margins are still thin because they're running continuous model updates on relatively expensive hardware. I want to see them pressure-test a more aggressive caching strategy before Series B conversations heat up. Priya flagged a potential partnership with a grid operator in the PJM region that could be transformative if the data-sharing terms are workable.

Keeping this on my close-watch list through end of year. The thesis here — that AI-native MRV tooling becomes table stakes for corporate compliance — still feels right. Execution is what matters now.
