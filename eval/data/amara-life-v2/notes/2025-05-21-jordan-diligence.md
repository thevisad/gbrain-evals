Spent most of today in diligence mode on the Jordan deal. The team's carbon accounting methodology is genuinely novel — they're capturing Scope 3 emissions at a granularity I haven't seen from a Series A company before. Still, a few things are nagging at me.

The GPU cluster cost assumptions feel optimistic. They're modeling inference costs as if the current spot-market rates are durable, which they almost certainly aren't over a five-year horizon. I flagged this to [Sarah Chen](people/sarah-chen) since she's been tracking the hyperscaler pricing dynamics closely through her own portfolio. Curious to get her read before our IC memo goes out Thursday.

On the positive side, the founder's background in grid optimization is a genuine differentiator. Most climate-AI teams I see are either strong on the ML side or the domain side, rarely both. Jordan feels like a real exception.

Still need to pressure-test the go-to-market timeline with a couple of reference customers. Will block tomorrow morning for those calls.
