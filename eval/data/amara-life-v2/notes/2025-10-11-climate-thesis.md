Had a long walk this morning trying to articulate why our climate thesis keeps drifting toward infrastructure rather than pure-play carbon markets. Talked it through with [Jordan Park](people/jordan-park) over coffee afterward, and I think I finally landed on it: the durable value in this transition isn't in the credits themselves — it's in the pipes that move electrons, data, and trust at scale.

AI is accelerating this realization faster than I expected. The compute buildout is enormous, and every hyperscaler is now a de facto energy procurer. That creates a genuine opening for climate-native infrastructure plays that serve both the power and the data layer simultaneously.

Jordan pushed back a bit — asked whether we're just rebranding grid investment as climate tech. Fair challenge. But I think the answer is no, provided we stay disciplined about additionality and emissions trajectory. The framing matters less than the outcome. We're still early in refining this, but the conviction is growing.
