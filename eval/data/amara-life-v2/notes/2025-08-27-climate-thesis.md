Been spending the last few weeks stress-testing our climate thesis heading into Q4. The core conviction hasn't shifted: the most durable opportunities sit at the intersection of compute infrastructure and physical-world decarbonization. Not climate tech as a category, but the enabling layer underneath it — the grid software, the industrial sensing, the power management that makes everything else possible.

What's changed is my read on timing. Eighteen months ago I was worried we were too early. Now I'm more worried about the herd. Every generalist fund is suddenly "climate-curious," which compresses valuations on the obvious bets and leaves the genuinely hard infrastructure plays undercapitalized.

That's actually where I want us to spend attention. The deals that require you to understand both a load-flow calculation and a transformer architecture aren't getting crowded. That's our edge if we're disciplined about it.

Need to write this up properly for the LP update. The thesis is getting sharper — I should reflect that.
