Been refining my thinking on the climate-AI infrastructure thesis heading into year-end. The core conviction hasn't shifted: the bottleneck isn't model capability anymore — it's the physical and energy infrastructure underneath it. Data centers are now one of the fastest-growing sources of electricity demand globally, and that creates a strange loop where AI is simultaneously our best tool for optimizing grid behavior and one of the primary stressors on it.

Had a sharp conversation with [Hannah Liu](people/hannah-liu) this week that helped me tighten the framing. She pushed back on whether "climate AI infra" is actually a coherent category or just two adjacent theses wearing the same jacket. Fair challenge. My answer: the coherence comes from the capital stack — these deals require investors who can underwrite both software risk and long-duration physical asset risk simultaneously. Most funds can't hold both.

That's the wedge. That's why Halfway sits where it does. Still testing whether the market agrees.
