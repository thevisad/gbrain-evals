Spent the morning working through the Q1 climate infrastructure outlook that [Priya Menon](people/priya-menon) flagged last week. A few things stood out. The report is bullish on grid-edge AI — demand response, real-time load forecasting — which tracks with what we've been hearing from founders, but the capital deployment figures feel optimistic given how slowly interconnection queues are actually moving. [Tobias Rhein](people/tobias-rhein) pushed back on the same section in our thread this morning, and I think he's right to be skeptical.

What the report gets correct is the compression happening in compute costs for inference at the edge. That tailwind is real and it's changing the unit economics for a whole category of climate-monitoring companies we've been watching.

Overall: useful temperature check, but I'd weight the on-the-ground operator conversations more heavily than the macro projections. Markets are moving faster than any quarterly report can cleanly capture right now.
