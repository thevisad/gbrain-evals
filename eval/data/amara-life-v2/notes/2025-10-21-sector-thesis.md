Been sitting with a question all week: at what point does AI infrastructure *become* climate infrastructure? The line keeps blurring for me in useful ways.

The thesis I keep returning to is that the next decade of compute buildout is essentially an energy story. Who controls the power, the cooling, the siting decisions — that's who shapes the emissions curve. We're not going to decarbonize AI by optimizing model efficiency alone, as important as that work is. We need capital flowing into the physical layer: next-gen cooling systems, grid interconnection software, load-shifting orchestration, co-located renewables.

What excites me about this moment is that founders are finally building *across* the stack rather than in one vertical. I'm seeing more pitches where the hardware, energy procurement, and software intelligence are designed together from day one. That integration is where durable defensibility lives.

Still refining how we frame this publicly, but internally the thesis is settling: climate and AI infra aren't parallel bets — they're converging into the same bet.
