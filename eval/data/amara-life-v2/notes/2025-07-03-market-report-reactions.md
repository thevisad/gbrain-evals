Spent the morning digesting the Q2 climate-infra market report that [Delia Wratten](people/delia-wratten) flagged last week. A few things stood out. Hyperscaler capex commitments to low-carbon compute are finally tracking ahead of 2024 projections — encouraging, though I'd caution against reading linearity into it. The section on grid interconnection backlogs felt undercooked; that's still the silent killer for any data center siting thesis we're running.

Called [Kofi Mensah-Addo](people/kofi-mensah-addo) afterward to gut-check the cooling-tech segment. He pushed back on the report's framing around dry-cooled facilities, and I think he's right to be skeptical.

Worth noting: the report lists [Rhea Solano](people/rhea-solano) as still serving as CEO of Verdant Compute, which tracks — she's been a steady hand there and the operational continuity has been a quiet confidence signal for that part of our portfolio. We'll revisit assumptions at the September LP update.
