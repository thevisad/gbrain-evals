Had a long walk this morning before calls and kept returning to a question I can't quite shake: are we funding climate infrastructure, or are we funding the infrastructure that *enables* climate infrastructure? The distinction matters more than it sounds.

Caught up with [Priya Patel](people/priya-patel) yesterday, and she pushed back on our framing in a way I found genuinely useful. Her point — that the grid coordination layer is as critical as the generation asset itself — recentered something I'd been drifting away from. We've been so drawn to the hardware stories lately that the software-defined grid thesis has gotten quieter in our internal conversations than it deserves.

I want to revisit our climate thesis doc before the LP update in July. Not to change direction, but to sharpen the language around why AI infrastructure and climate infrastructure are converging rather than competing for capital. That story is getting easier to tell. I should make sure we're telling it clearly.
