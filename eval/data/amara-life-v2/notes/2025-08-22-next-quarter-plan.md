Sitting down to map out Q4 priorities before the August lull fully evaporates. The through-line this quarter is clear: we need to sharpen our thesis at the intersection of AI infrastructure and grid-scale energy. Too many decks are landing in our inbox that treat these as separate bets — they're not anymore. Compute demand is reshaping power procurement, and I want us to be the firm that can speak fluently to both sides of that equation.

Concretely, I'm planning to deepen relationships in the transmission and storage space, and push harder on a few AI datacenter deals where the energy story is differentiated. I also want to carve out time to revisit our portfolio's collective emissions footprint — we talk about climate constantly in diligence but less rigorously post-investment.

One personal commitment: I'm going to write more. Shorter, more frequent notes like this one. Thinking out loud is underrated. The best investment intuitions I've had started as half-formed observations, not polished memos.
