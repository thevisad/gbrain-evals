Had a long call with [Elena Rossi](people/elena-rossi) this afternoon going through the competitive dynamics in climate AI infra. What struck me most was how quickly the mid-tier players are getting squeezed. The hyperscalers are pushing down from the top with bundled compute offerings, and a new wave of specialized edge-inference startups is clawing up from below. The space in between — where a lot of our current portfolio sits — is thinning faster than I'd like to admit.

Elena flagged two European players she's been watching that I hadn't tracked closely. Worth a deeper look before our next IC meeting.

My honest read: the moats that felt durable eighteen months ago — proprietary cooling architectures, grid interconnect relationships — are holding, but only barely. Teams that also have strong software differentiation on top of the hardware story are pulling ahead. Going to spend next week stress-testing our existing positions against that lens before we consider any new checks in this category.
