Been refining my thinking on where climate and AI infrastructure actually converge — not just overlap rhetorically, but create genuine compounding value. The short version: the interesting companies aren't building AI *for* climate or climate *for* AI. They're building shared physical infrastructure where the two demands reinforce each other.

Think power-dense compute facilities co-located with firm renewable generation. Or grid optimization software that treats data center load as a dispatchable asset. The capital requirements are enormous, but so is the durability of the revenue streams once the contracts are in place.

What I keep coming back to is that both sectors punish premature scaling and reward operational discipline. Founders who understand that — who've actually run something with a physical footprint — are a different category of interesting to me than pure software people pivoting toward the space.

Still testing this thesis against the deal flow we're seeing. But the directionality feels right.
